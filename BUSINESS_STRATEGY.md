# LearnRift — Business Strategy & Go-To-Market

_Last updated: 2026-06-05. Owner: Kumar Shivam (founder + admin + lead creator)._

This is the operating plan to make LearnRift **profitable this internship/placement season**. It assumes the platform model already built: creators publish courses, learners pay **per-month** for access, the platform (you) keeps **~30% commission**, and on **your own courses you keep 100%** (your 30% admin cut + the 70% creator share).

---

## 1. The one-line strategy

> **Be the affordable, structured, India-first internship & placement-prep platform — launched right now, into the season, on the channels where students already hang out, led by your own high-demand courses.**

The timing is the whole game: **May–August is peak internship-prep season** for Indian college students (2nd/3rd years targeting summer internships + SDE roles). Demand is high and intent is urgent. You don't need a big audience to profit — you need the *right* courses in front of *high-intent* students *now*.

---

## 2. Why you can win (and where the money actually is)

- **Your real profit engine early is YOUR OWN courses, not the 30% commission.** On your own content you keep 100%. The break-even math (below) shows you're profitable at a few dozen subscribers if *you* are the creator. Commission from other creators is upside that compounds later.
- **Per-month access = recurring revenue.** A student prepping for 3 months at ₹250/mo = ₹750 LTV, and they'll churn out naturally after placements — which is fine; the next batch arrives every season.
- **You're cheaper + more focused than the incumbents.** Big players (Scaler, Coding Ninjas/Naukri, GFG premium) are expensive; free YouTube is unstructured. Your wedge: **structured + affordable + season-timed + Indian-context**.

You currently have **Data Structures**. That's the anchor. Build outward in this order — these cover ~80% of what internship/placement prep needs, and they're fast for *you* to produce (so you keep 100%):

### Tier 1 — build these in the next 2–3 weeks (highest demand, highest intent)
1. **DSA Problem-Solving Patterns** (sliding window, two-pointers, recursion/backtracking, DP, graphs) — company-tagged, LeetCode-style. This is what every SDE-intern aspirant pays for.
2. **CS Core Fundamentals** — **OS, DBMS, Computer Networks, OOPs.** Every interview + every aptitude+tech round asks these. High volume, evergreen.
3. **Aptitude + Logical Reasoning** — for the first-round filters at TCS/Infosys/Wipro/Cognizant (mass recruiters). Huge top-of-funnel audience.

### Tier 2 — next 4–6 weeks
4. **Low-Level + High-Level System Design (basics)** — for product-company SDE interviews.
5. **Language for Placements** — C++ / Java / Python crash course (pick the one your audience uses).
6. **Resume + HR/Behavioral Interview prep + Mock Interviews** — a cheap-to-make differentiator most coding platforms ignore.

### Tier 3 — breadth (recruit other creators here → your 30% commission)
7. Company-specific tracks ("Crack Amazon/Microsoft SDE Intern").
8. SQL, Git, web-dev / ML basics for relevant internships.
9. GATE/CS-theory for the higher-end audience.

**Production tip:** quizzes (now with the rich editor + images) are your retention + credibility tool — add a graded quiz to every module. "Mock test" courses (pure quizzes) are cheap to produce and very sellable in aptitude/CS-fundamentals.

## 4. Go-to-market — where to post FIRST (in order)

Indian students live in **WhatsApp/Telegram + Instagram/YouTube + Reddit/LinkedIn**. Lead with **free value → funnel to paid**. Don't spread thin; nail 2–3 channels first.

### Launch sequence (week 1)
1. **College WhatsApp & Telegram groups** — #1 channel, zero cost, highest intent. Branch groups, placement-prep groups, coding-club groups, your own college network. Post a *free* lesson/mini-course link, not a hard sell.
2. **Telegram placement-prep channels** — there are large existing channels (placement materials, off-campus drives). Get featured / cross-post / run a free resource that links back.
3. **LinkedIn** — post genuinely useful placement content (a DSA pattern, an OS cheat-sheet) 3–4×/week, soft-CTA to LearnRift. Students *and* the recruiters they follow are here.

### Week 2–4 (compounding channels)
4. **Instagram Reels + YouTube Shorts** — 30–60s "1 DSA pattern / 1 OS concept / 1 aptitude trick" clips → bio link to the course. EdTech in India runs on Reels. Repurpose one concept into 3 clips.
5. **Reddit** — `r/developersIndia`, `r/leetcode`, `r/cscareerquestionsIndia`, your college subreddit. Give value first (answer questions, share a free resource); Reddit punishes pure promotion.
6. **Discord coding servers** + **X/Twitter** tech-India community.

### Partnerships (week 3+) — the force multiplier
7. **College placement cells & coding clubs** — offer a **bulk/group deal** or free access for the club in exchange for promotion to the batch. One club = hundreds of high-intent students.
8. **Student ambassadors / referral program** — give referrers a free month or cash; students share aggressively during placement season.

> **Acquisition principle:** give away a **free aptitude or DSA-basics mini-course** (and free preview lessons on every paid course) as the top of funnel. Capture them free, convert with structure + quizzes + the urgency of the season.

## 5. Monetization — how to make maximum profit RIGHT NOW

### Pricing (tie to the unit economics)
- **Single course:** ₹150–₹300 / month. (Per-month framing: price it as "monthly access," not full-course price.)
- **"Internship Prep Bundle" (all-access):** ₹399–₹599 / month — DSA + CS fundamentals + aptitude + resume. **This is your hero SKU** — higher average revenue, simpler choice for a stressed student.
- **Launch offer (season urgency):** "Placement-season price — locked for your prep months." Time-boxed discount to drive sign-ups now.

### Break-even (from the unit-economics analysis)
After Razorpay (~2.5%), you net **~27% of gross sales**. Break-even = monthly cost ÷ 0.27.
- **Lean infra (~₹3.5k/mo): break even at ~40–65 paying subscribers** at ₹200–300/mo.
- And on **your own courses you keep 100%**, so your own content reaches profit *much* sooner.

**Maximum-profit levers, in order of impact:**
1. **Produce the Tier-1 courses yourself** → keep 100%, not 30%. This is the single biggest profit lever right now.
2. **Sell the bundle, not just single courses** → higher revenue per student.
3. **Run lean infra** (see §6) → lower break-even (≈₹6k/mo saved by right-sizing the VM).
4. **Free funnel + referrals** → cheap acquisition during a high-intent season.
5. Recruit other creators for breadth later → 30% commission scales the catalog without your production time.

### 90-day money plan
- **Weeks 1–2:** ship Tier-1 courses; right-size infra; launch in WhatsApp/Telegram/LinkedIn; free mini-course live. **Goal: first 50 paying subscribers.**
- **Weeks 3–6:** Reels/Shorts engine; Tier-2 courses; club partnerships + referrals. **Goal: 150–300 subscribers, clearly profitable.**
- **Weeks 7–12:** recruit 2–3 external creators (commission catalog); company-specific tracks; double down on the channels that converted. **Goal: recurring profit + a content flywheel.**

## 6. Reliability & Scaling — solving the "frequent crashes" (and the autoscaling question)

Straight talk: **the crashes you're seeing are almost certainly NOT a capacity problem, and autoscaling will not fix them.** Your own load test showed the current VM (e2-standard-4) handling **500 concurrent users at ~38% CPU** — you have massive headroom. So the dips/"crashes" come from these, in likely order:

### Root causes
1. **Deploy downtime (the #1 cause of the dips).** `deploy.sh` currently **stops all 14 services, then runs `npm ci` + the Next.js build, then restarts** — a deliberate **1–3 minute outage on every deploy**. Every push = a dip + 502s. We deployed several times today → that's the dips you saw.
2. **Razorpay API blips → "payment gateway 502."** `/create-order` returns a 502 when Razorpay's API call times out. Network-level, intermittent.
3. **Possible per-service memory restarts.** PM2 restarts a service if it exceeds its `max_memory_restart` (currently 256 MB/service, 768 MB frontend). Under load a service can cross 256 MB → PM2 restarts it → a brief blip. On your 16 GB box these limits are needlessly tight.

### The fix sequence (do these — they're the real solution)
1. **Zero-/low-downtime deploys.** Rework `deploy.sh` so the **backend stays up during install+build** and services are **reloaded** (not all deleted at once). Kills the deploy dips. *(Needs careful implementation — offered below.)*
2. **Raise PM2 memory limits** in `ecosystem.config.cjs` (e.g., frontend 1.5 GB, services 512 MB) — you have 16 GB; stop restarting services that are merely busy.
3. **Retry Razorpay order creation** (2–3 attempts) before returning 502.
4. **Monitoring + alerts: already on** (Cloud Ops Agent + uptime check). Watch `pm2 list` restart counts — if they climb, a specific service is crashing and we fix that bug from the logs.
5. **Right-size the VM** (e2-standard-4 → e2-medium): cuts cost ~₹6k/mo and is still far above your real load. Scale back up only when traffic demands.

### Autoscaling — the honest answer
- **You don't need it for capacity** (proven by the load test) and **it won't stop crashes** — a Managed Instance Group just clones a crashing VM into more crashing VMs.
- Autoscaling's only real benefits are **high availability** (if one VM dies, others serve) and **traffic spikes beyond one big box**. Worth it **later**, once you have revenue and real traffic.
- **When you're ready (revenue-justified):** HTTP(S) Load Balancer + a Managed Instance Group of 2+ identical VMs + Cloud CDN, with deploys switched to a **golden image or startup script** (the current SSH-to-one-VM deploy can't reach autoscaled VMs), the **rate limiter moved to Redis** (so limits stay global), and the **DB connection pool capped** (so N VMs don't exhaust Supabase). That's a multi-day project — **do the stability fixes first.**

**Bottom line on reliability:** stability fixes (#1–#5) eliminate the crashes you actually have, cheaply. Autoscaling is a "later, for HA" item, not a fix for the current dips.

## 7. Metrics to track (weekly)
- **Acquisition:** sign-ups, free→paid conversion %, traffic by channel.
- **Revenue:** active paying subscribers, MRR, ARPU, your-courses vs commission split.
- **Retention:** month-2 renewal %, churn.
- **Content:** course completion %, quiz pass rates (credibility signal).
- **Reliability:** uptime %, PM2 restart counts, deploy-downtime minutes, p95 latency.

## 8. Risks & how to handle them
- **Seasonality:** internship demand is peaky. Counter with evergreen CS-fundamentals/aptitude + a fresh batch every season + annual (not just monthly) options later.
- **Strong free/cheap competition** (YouTube, Striver A2Z, GFG): differentiate on **structure + quizzes/mock tests + affordability + Indian-context + support**, not on "more videos."
- **Single-VM fragility:** mitigated by §6 (then HA when revenue justifies).
- **Trust/payments:** you've now fixed the "paid but no access" bug — keep the reconcile + monitoring tight; payment trust is existential for a paid platform.
